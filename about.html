Iâ€™m a tech-savvy millennial that has always loved creating pretty things and helping people. Whether that was by designing locker signs for the football players --- I cheered for every Friday night in high school, or making beautiful presentations for my college assignments, if I got to be creative, I was all over it. Little did I know that what I was doing graphic design.

Growing up, design was not an option. I was set to find a stable salary job and pursue a masters in the medical industry, but my status as the black sheep of the family is solidified since I am the only one without a post-graduate degree. After college, I spent four years in corporate America. I bounced around during that time between industries with the most time spent in real estate, but I never found a role that truly fulfilled me.

No matter what the job was, I was always willing to help with the creative tasks. I created invitations for company functions, plus marketing flyers and social media posts for the communities I worked in and even started dabbling in web design. Developing my design and marketing skills or conquering a new creative software was my escape.

Eventually, I came to a point where I decided to leave my steady 9 to 5 to follow where my heart lies, design. I set out to grow my graphic and web design skills while freelancing for small businesses and entrepreneurs. I have helped clients build their online presence through professional websites and social media platforms. Having the ability to help businesses all over the country, improve their brand recognition through online marketing in this digital age has been my bliss.

Currently, I work in all aspects of marketing and design, but I am striving towards making web design and branding my full-time focus. The joy I can create for clients when I bring their story to life makes my heart happy and is the most important reason I do what I do. I have come a long way since making locker signs, but now I am a cheerleader of brands.
